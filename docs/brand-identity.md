# Brand Identity

## Core Identity

**Pasta Lab & Smoked Flavors** is a contemporary pasta bistro in Tallinn where handmade fresh pasta meets smoked, fermented, and slow-worked local ingredients.

Primary payoff:
- Fresh pasta. Smoke. Ferment. Fire.

Secondary brand line:
- Italian craft, Baltic character.

This is not a trattoria, not cold fine dining, and not generic fusion. It should read as a warm, precise, editorial, intimate, premium-but-welcoming bistro.

## Positioning

The venue is imagined as a former industrial space near the port of Tallinn, remade as a bistro-lab.

The food direction combines Italian pasta craft with Northern technique and Baltic ingredients:
- smoked butter
- fermented vegetables
- mushrooms
- dill
- rye
- charred onion
- living acidity
- reduced broths

The point is not nostalgia. It is memory transformed into character.

## Audience

- Tallinn locals looking for a distinctive dinner place
- Design-aware visitors who want a memorable but grounded dining experience
- Guests who value craft, seasonality, texture, and a clear point of view

## Tone Of Voice

- Warm
- Precise
- Sensory
- Restrained
- Atmospheric
- Believable

Avoid:
- “authentic Italian experience” language
- generic luxury language
- tourist-brochure tone
- overstatement
- food clichés that flatten the concept

Copy should describe ingredients, technique, and room mood clearly. It should feel composed, not promotional.

## Visual Direction

Editorial warmth, Nordic shadow, material textures, cinematic intimacy.

Key cues:
- dark wood
- matte copper
- linen
- smoked glass
- ceramic in cream, ash, and charcoal
- flour
- steam
- butter sheen
- rye
- herbs
- subtle smoke

The palette should suggest heat, wood, steel, and smoke without tipping into rustic nostalgia or generic fine-dining polish.

## Logo System

The brand now uses a code-based SVG system instead of the fragile exported raster/script asset.

Primary wordmark:
- Used in the site header
- Horizontal, compact, and readable
- Best for navigation and general UI

Signature mark:
- Used in the footer and other branded editorial moments
- More atmospheric, with a stacked title treatment and a secondary line
- Best for House pages, section openers, and premium brand moments

Monogram:
- Used for favicon and compact app/icon needs
- Keep it simple, dark, and legible at small sizes
- The compact PL monogram now also powers the SVG favicon, PNG fallbacks, and Apple touch icon so browser identity stays coherent across platforms

All variants should stay warm, precise, and slightly classic. They should feel like Pasta Lab & Smoked Flavors rather than a generic luxury mark or a decorative script logo.

## Brand Personality

Should feel:
- sensory
- material
- intelligent
- crafted
- intimate
- precise

Should not feel:
- folkloric rustic
- generic luxury
- cliché Italian nostalgia
- random fusion
- generic industrial hipster

## Signature Anchors

Core dishes and references to preserve in future implementation:
- Tagliolini al burro affumicato
- Ravioli di patata fermentata
- Pappardelle ai funghi baltici
- Tortelloni al quark e aneto
- Gnocchi tostati con cavolo fermentato

Hospitality rituals:
- warm bread with whipped smoked butter on seating
- visible or implied handmade pasta craft
- short curated menu
- soft amber lighting
- optional pairings beyond wine: kombucha, kefir, infusions, natural wines, cider

## Color Direction

Current palette directions remain:
- `cream` for main surfaces
- `ink` for typography and contrast
- `smoke` for secondary copy
- `copper` as the signature accent
- `olive` as a natural secondary tone

These colors should feel warm and material, not decorative.
