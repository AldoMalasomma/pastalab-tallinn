# Menu Concept

## Culinary Logic

The menu is built around a single idea: pasta as the main craft, and Baltic/Northern ingredients as the source of character.

Pasta provides structure, comfort, and repetition the kitchen can perfect. Smoke and fermentation provide depth, tension, and memory. Baltic ingredients keep the concept grounded in place rather than nostalgia.

## Roles In The Concept

- Pasta: the technical anchor and the brand’s primary craft signal
- Smoke: warmth, depth, and a subtle edge
- Fermentation: brightness, tension, and living acidity
- Baltic ingredients: local identity, seasonality, and specificity

## Current Signature Dish Anchors

These dishes should remain concept references even as the menu evolves:

- Tagliolini al burro affumicato
- Ravioli di patata fermentata
- Pappardelle ai funghi baltici
- Tortelloni al quark e aneto
- Gnocchi tostati con cavolo fermentato

## Intended Menu Structure

The menu should stay short, curated, and believable. A compact structure works best:

1. Small plates
   - Bread, butter, vegetables, snacks, and a few opening bites
2. Fresh pasta
   - The core of the offer and the main reason to visit
3. Dessert
   - Small, seasonal, and not overdesigned
4. Pairings
   - Wine plus lower-alcohol or non-wine options

## Pairing Direction

Pairings may include:

- kombucha
- kefir
- infusions
- natural wine
- dry cider

These should feel deliberate, not trend-driven.

## Menu Rules

- Keep it short.
- Keep it seasonal.
- Keep it specific.
- Keep it realistic for a premium-but-welcoming Tallinn bistro.
- Avoid bloat, novelty stacking, and “something for everyone” thinking.

## Implementation Note

The menu should be written so it can live comfortably on the website, in print, and in future reservation or tasting materials without changing its core logic.
